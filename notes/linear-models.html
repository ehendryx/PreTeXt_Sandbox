    Section Section 1.1: Linear Models

    A model describes a relationship between variables. This relationship can be represented in a variety of ways:
    • with a table of values
    • with a graph
    • with an equation
    Ex: A taxi out of Dulles Airport in Washington, DC, charges a traveler an initial fee of $2.00, plus $1.50 for each mile traveled.
    1. Complete the table of values showing the charge, \(C\text{,}\) for a trip of \(n\) miles.
      \(n\) 0 5 10 15 20 25
      \(C\)
    2. Graph the relationship.
    1. Write an equation for the charge, \(C\text{,}\) in terms of the number of miles traveled, \(n\text{.}\)
    2. What is the charge for a trip to Mount Vernon, 35 miles from the airport? Illustrate the answer on your graph.
    3. If a ride to the National Institutes of Health (NIH) costs $42.50, how far is it from the airport to the NIH? Illustrate the answer on your graph.
    The of a graph are the points where the graph crosses the axes.
    • The is the point \((0,y)\) found by setting and solving for y.
    • The is the point \((x,0)\) found by setting and solving for x.
    Ex: Find the intercepts of the graph in the airport example above. What do these intercepts represent?
    Graphing a Line Using the “Intercept Method”:
    1. Find the \(x\)- and \(y\)-intercepts.
    2. Plot the intercepts.
    3. Choose a value of \(x\) and find a third point on the line to make it a bit easier to make the line straight.
    4. Draw a line through the points.

    Linear equations can also be written in what is sometimes called general form:
    Ex: Five pounds of body fat is equivalent to 16,000 calories. Carol can burn 600 calories per hour bicycling and 400 calories per hour swimming.
    1. Write an equation in general form that relates the number of hours, \(x\text{,}\) of cycling and the number of hours, \(y\text{,}\) of swimming Carol needs to perform to burn 16,000 calories.
    2. Find the intercepts and use them to sketch the graph of the linear equation.
    3. What do the intercepts tell us about Carol’s exercise program?
    4. Is the graph you drew in part (2) increasing or decreasing?
    Ex: Frank plants a dozen corn seedlings, each 6 inches tall. With plenty of water and sunlight they will grow approximately 2 inches per day.
    1. Complete the table of values for the height, \(h\text{,}\) of the seedlings after \(t\) days.
      \(t\) 0 5 10 15 20 25
      \(h\)
    2. Write an equation for the height of the seedlings in terms of the number of days since they were planted.
    3. Find the intercepts of the graph.
    4. Graph the equation.
    5. What do the intercepts tell us about the problem?
