    Suppose we want to fit the exponential function \(f(x) = ab^{x}\) to two known data points. To do so, we can use the “ratio method”:
    1. Plug the coordinates of each point into the exponential function to write two equations in terms of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)
    2. Divide one equation by the other to eliminate \(a\) and solve for \(b\text{.}\)
    3. Substitute \(b\) into either equation from Step 1 and solve for \(a\text{.}\)

    An is a sequence of equal payments or deposits made at equal time intervals.
    • If you make \(n\) payments per year for \(t\) years into an annuity that pays interest rate \(r\) compounded \(n\) times per year, the future value, \(FV\text{,}\) of the annuity is
      \begin{equation*} FV = \frac{P\left[\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}- 1\right]}{\frac{r}{n}}, \end{equation*}
      where each payment is \(P\) dollars.
    • If you wish to receive \(n\) payments per year for \(t\) years from a fund that earns interest rate \(r\) compounded \(n\) times per year, the present value, \(PV\text{,}\) of the annuity must be
      \begin{equation*} PV = \frac{P\left[1 - \left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{-nt}\right]}{\frac{r}{n}}, \end{equation*}
      where each payment is \(P\) dollars.
